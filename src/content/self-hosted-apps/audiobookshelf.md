A lot of self-hosted media tools feel like chores with a web UI. Audiobookshelf does not. It solves a narrow problem, does it well, and does not demand a weekend of tuning before it becomes useful.

## Why it works

The interface is clean, the metadata handling is good enough to trust, and mobile playback feels like a real product instead of a side project with a progress bar.

## What I like most

```bash
Docker deployment is simple.
Library organization is predictable.
Remote access works well behind a reverse proxy.
```

If you collect audiobooks or podcasts and want them under your control, Audiobookshelf is one of the easiest self-hosted wins.
